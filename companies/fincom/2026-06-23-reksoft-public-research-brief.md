## Short Verdict

Reksoft is not a small or purely 1C-focused vendor. Publicly they present themselves as a large Russian enterprise technology group: business/IT consulting, ERP and business applications, SAP/Oracle import substitution, industrial automation, MES, data/BI, AI, cybersecurity, infrastructure, and custom software development.

They are a competitor at the broad services/integration layer. They can also be a partner or channel if Fincom has a concrete product/module/methodology that helps them win or de-risk enterprise transformation projects. If they are brought in as external evaluators rather than approached as partners, treat them primarily as an expert due-diligence party with possible conflict of interest.

Important nuance added after review: their site shows "solutions" and platforms, but does not clearly read like a product company with one defensible, repeatable flagship product. The public positioning is closer to integrator/consultancy with packaged accelerators and reference solutions. This weakens the concern that they already own a direct product substitute, but strengthens the concern that they can absorb, rebuild, or repackage a narrow solution if Fincom's edge is not protected and well-positioned.

## What They Are

- Founded in 1991; official site says 35 years on the market, 1500+ employees, 150+ management consultants, 9 industry expertise centers, and 76 of the top 100 Russian companies as clients.
- They position around "digital transformation from concept to implementation" and complex systems, not only software outsourcing.
- Their industries on the site include mining/metallurgy, oil and gas, transport/logistics, banking/finance, insurance, retail/wholesale, government/social initiatives, agriculture, and energy.
- Leadership/team positioning includes Reksoft Consulting, custom development, industrial automation, cloud/data, security, mining/metallurgy, MES, and other business lines.

## ERP / 1C Answer

Public answer: they do not "do everything only on 1C", but 1C is a major part of their business-applications offer.

Evidence from their business-applications page:

- They describe an enterprise management ecosystem across finance, production, HR, and assets.
- Their portfolio explicitly includes ERP, EPM, HRM, and EAM on Russian platforms: 1C, Galaktika, Optimacros, Turbo, and others.
- They offer migration from Western systems such as SAP and Oracle.
- They explicitly list 1C:ERP, 1C:ERP УХ, 1C:ЗУП КОРП, and migration from 1C:УПП.
- They claim 20+ vendor partnerships and 1000+ developers across platforms, and say they are not limited to one vendor.

Interpretation: if Fincom competes by saying "we can implement ERP", Reksoft is much stronger. If Fincom brings a specialized vertical solution, data layer, AI automation layer, implementation accelerator, or domain-specific module, Reksoft could consume it inside larger programs.

## Competencies Map

- Business and IT consulting: strategy, operating models, process reengineering, architecture, transformation office.
- Business applications: ERP, EPM, HRM, EAM, import substitution, 1C, Galaktika, Optimacros, Turbo, SAP/Oracle migration.
- Industrial: industrial automation, MES, mining/metallurgy, oil and gas, energy, objective control, alarm management, HMI, open/underground mining systems.
- AI: AI-first strategy, LLM/AI agents, process selection and ROI, AI applications, document/audio/video processing, assistants, analytics, prediction, digital twins, AI infrastructure and AI Ops.
- Data: BI, analytics, cross-functional data processing, data platforms.
- Software engineering: custom development, microservice platform, enterprise systems, dedicated development/testing/support.
- Security/infrastructure: information security and IT infrastructure services are top-level service lines.

## Public Product / Solution Signals

Reksoft lists ready platforms/solutions, including:

- baggage handling system for airports;
- Reksoft AI Compliance;
- Reksoft DAM 2.0;
- automated open-pit and underground mining management systems;
- HMI and alarm-management solutions;
- objective-control systems;
- microservice platform for insurance.

This means they are not only selling bodies or ERP implementation; they are assembling a solution portfolio.

However, the public site does not make a strong product-company impression. The named solutions look heterogeneous and attached to industry programs/cases rather than a single repeatable product line with clear SKU/pricing/public implementation pattern. Treat "they have solutions" as "they package delivery assets", not as proof that they have a mature competing product in Fincom's target niche.

## Portfolio Coherence Concern

The site places very different stories side by side: industrial automation / mining systems / robotics-like automation on one side, and insurance software / banking / retail / DAM / AI compliance on the other.

Interpretation:

- Positive read: they are a broad enterprise integrator with multiple practices and can enter many large accounts.
- Negative read: the portfolio may be opportunistic and service-led; public cases may demonstrate delivery breadth more than product depth.
- For Fincom, this is both risk and opportunity. Risk: broad integrators can say "we can do that too" even without owning a deep product. Opportunity: if they lack a focused product in a niche, they may need a credible narrow component, especially if it helps them sell into ERP/industrial/operations transformation.

## Public Financial / Reputation Signals

Latest public market-ranking signal found:

- RAEX 2025 ranking of Russian ICT groups, based on 2024 results, lists Reksoft at rank 19 with 6,839,893 thousand RUB IT revenue for 2024 versus 4,302,683 thousand RUB for 2023: +59.0% growth. It lists 1,474 specialists and group participant type with 6 companies.
- CNews Analytics 2026 data/analytics ranking lists Reksoft at rank 11 for data-analytics projects, with 1,192 million RUB revenue in 2025 versus 843 million RUB in 2024: +41.4%; specialization marked as integration; named customers include GAZ, STT, Avtodata, Terra Tech.
- Forbes Club / Practicum article by Andrey Semerkin, published 2024-09-23 and modified 2025-07-24, frames Reksoft's post-2022 expansion as integration of business and expert teams rather than simple acquisitions. The article says Reksoft exited T1 through a management buyout at the end of 2021, received fast investment from Interros in April 2022, brought in teams/expertise associated with Accenture, VIST, Schneider Electric, Siemens, Aveva, RNT, and Russian EPAM, and grew 2023 group revenue to 3.35bn RUB. It also reports 2022 revenue of 1.1bn RUB and net loss of 236m RUB.
- Reksoft's own site lists 1500+ employees, 150+ management consultants, 9 industry expertise centers, 76 of top 100 Russian companies as clients, and a broad list of licenses/partnerships including FSB/FSTEC licenses, ISO 9001, 1C partnership, Astra, Kaspersky and others.

Interpretation:

- Financial-health read is positive on revenue scale/growth: this does not look like a distressed small vendor.
- Forbes adds a useful trajectory signal: 2022 loss followed by sharp revenue growth in 2023-2025 public ranking data. That looks more like aggressive expansion/integration after a market shock than classic stable-product SaaS economics.
- But the public data found is mostly revenue/ranking data, not consolidated audited financial statements with profit, cash, debt, receivables, customer concentration, or litigation exposure. Treat financial strength as plausible, not fully underwritten.
- RAEX explicitly says its ranking source is participant-provided data. Good market signal, not equivalent to audited public-company disclosure.

Public/structural caution signals:

- Ownership / sanctions spillover: public secondary sources connect Reksoft to Interros / Vladimir Potanin's group. Potanin and Interros are subject to Western sanctions according to public sources. This does not automatically mean Reksoft itself is sanctioned, but it matters for international/compliance-sensitive counterparties and for any Positive/Fincom assessment involving non-Russian money, vendors, infrastructure, or reputational risk.
- Ownership changes: Forbes cites Rusprofile for the 2022 structure, including 20% for an Interros structure, and Interfax for an April 2024 change where Oman-registered Desert Spark SPC reportedly became a 25% shareholder. Forbes says Alexander Egorov with management remained above 50%, while Interros Capital and Filin Investment moved to 15% and 9.22% respectively.
- Group structure: Reksoft's official IT-accreditation page lists several accredited companies in the group: ООО «Рексофт», ООО «Рексофт.РУ», ООО «Рексофт.МСК», ООО «Рексофт.Лабс», ООО «РНТ», ООО «Рексофт Интеграция». This is normal for a group, but for diligence it means single-entity financials may not describe the whole economic reality.
- Acquisition / roll-up profile: public materials emphasize consolidation of former Accenture, VIST, Schneider/Siemens/Aveva, RNT, EPAM and other teams. This is a strength for expertise and relationships, but also a signal of integration complexity and service-led breadth. It explains why the portfolio can contain both industrial/mining systems and insurance/enterprise software: this is a multi-practice group assembled around post-2022 market exits, not a narrowly focused product house.
- Public PR profile: the Russian Wikipedia page for Reksoft is marked as promotional and, at the time checked, had a deletion discussion notice. Do not use that page as authoritative proof; use it only as a map to other sources. The signal is that their public footprint is polished/PR-heavy.
- Product ambiguity: their site claims product/solution assets such as Reksoft AI Hub, AI Compliance, DAM, mining management systems, etc., but public evidence still reads more like packaged delivery assets than one defensible flagship product company.

Quick red/yellow/green read:

- Green: revenue scale and growth in RAEX; visible enterprise client/industry presence; licenses/accreditations; strong delivery/team narrative.
- Yellow: revenue rankings are not the same as profitability/cash-flow proof; self-reported ranking inputs; group of multiple legal entities; ownership/sanctions spillover; broad service-led portfolio.
- Red: no direct public red flag found in quick search for bankruptcy/litigation/distress, but this was not a full legal due diligence pass through paid контрагент databases, arbitration case files, FSSP, or SPARK/Контур.

## Strategic Fit For Fincom

Possible cooperation lanes:

- Module supplier: Fincom solution becomes a specialized component in Reksoft's enterprise programs.
- Expert validator: Reksoft opinion could validate whether Fincom's positioning is credible for enterprise ERP/operations buyers.
- Channel / prime-contractor route: Reksoft owns the large account and implementation risk; Fincom supplies a narrow differentiated capability.
- Technical due diligence route: Positive Technologies or another investor uses Reksoft people to assess Fincom's architecture, implementation maturity, and enterprise fit.

Main risk:

- Reksoft is broad enough to decide "we can build this ourselves" unless Fincom's edge is clearly packaged, defensible, and fast to adopt.
- If the discussion is generic, Fincom will look like a weaker services vendor. The pitch must be specific: what they get that is cheaper/faster/lower-risk than internal build.

## Expert Evaluation Risk

Clarified scenario: Fincom may not be choosing to approach Reksoft; rather, Positive Technologies / Viktor may bring Reksoft people in as outside experts to answer whether Fincom has real potential.

This is useful but risky:

- Useful: a respected enterprise integrator can validate whether the offer is credible in ERP/industrial/operations-heavy environments.
- Risky: Reksoft is not a neutral academic reviewer. They are a broad implementer with capabilities adjacent to ERP, industrial automation, AI, and custom development.
- Risky: if the session becomes a generic idea review, they may learn enough to dismiss, copy, or reframe the opportunity without needing Fincom.
- Risky: their incentives may be to say "interesting, but this should be done by a serious integrator", especially if the buyer/investor already trusts them.

Safe posture:

- Show evidence of traction, product thinking, architecture maturity, and business effect.
- Do not expose deep implementation internals, proprietary methods, prompts, data mappings, commercial model details, or customer-sensitive artifacts before NDA / purpose / attendees are clear.
- Ask for a defined evaluation frame in advance: what criteria are they judging, for whom, and what output will their opinion produce?
- Treat the meeting as investor/partner due diligence, not a friendly brainstorming session.

## Open Questions Before Any External Meeting

- Which exact Fincom solution should be shown to Reksoft or Positive Technologies?
