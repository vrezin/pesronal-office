## Goal

Shift the public profile from broad CTO / executive signaling to a clearer market signal:

- AI Product Engineer
- AI Solution Architect
- Fractional / Interim CTO for AI-first companies

The key rule: AI Enabler should appear as a service / capability description, not as the title itself.

`Fractional CTO | AI Product & Solution Architecture | AI-First Transformation for Stable Companies`

## Recommended About Section

I help companies turn AI from a promise into a working product and operational capability.

My focus is practical AI product engineering and solution architecture:

- AI-First development workflows;
- internal AI flows for teams and operations;
- agentic automation and workflow design;
- discovery -> pilot -> integration -> rollout;
- production quality, observability and measurable effect.

I work well in hands-on builder mode and can step in as a fractional / interim CTO when a stable company wants to adopt AI-first methods without startup chaos.

## Services / Positioning Lines

- AI-First development and delivery strategy
- AI workflow design for teams and internal operations
- AI productization and solution architecture
- Fractional / interim CTO for stable companies adopting AI-first

## What To Remove Or De-emphasize

- `200+ engineering organizations`
- broad “CTO / engineering executive” framing on the first screen
- generic scale bragging as the main identity
- startup-style language if the company is actually mature and wants disciplined AI adoption

## Experience Framing

Lead with current AI-enabled platform and delivery work, then keep large-scale engineering leadership as credibility support.

## Practical Rule

If a role is:

- stable enough to invest in AI-first change;
- serious about product, workflow and operational adoption;
- not just looking to “scramble something together”;

then `Fractional CTO` is a valid supporting frame.

If a role is a greenfield startup with chaos and no real operating model, do not use the fractional CTO frame as the primary promise.
