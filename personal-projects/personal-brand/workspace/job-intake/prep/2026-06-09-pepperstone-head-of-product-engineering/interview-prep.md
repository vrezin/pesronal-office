# Pepperstone Interview Prep

## What to say

- I am a hands-on product engineering leader, not an abstract executive.
- I have strong experience in regulated, high-load, customer-facing systems.
- I can lead distributed teams, delivery governance, reliability, and cross-functional alignment.
- AI is a practical efficiency layer for me, not the headline.
- I am genuinely interested in the role and in relocating to Cyprus.

## What to avoid

- Do not lead with biography length.
- Do not start with AI.
- Do not sound board-only or removed from operations.
- Do not over-explain why the market is unfair.
- Do not turn the interview into a story about being right in advance.

## Likely questions

- Why Pepperstone?
- Why are you interested in relocating?
- Tell us about your last relevant role.
- How do you lead distributed teams?
- How do you balance speed, quality, and governance?

## Strong answer shape

1. Context: the environment and scale.
2. Action: what you personally did.
3. Result: the measurable effect.
4. Relevance: why it matters for Pepperstone.

## Useful questions to ask them

- What are the top 3 problems this hire should solve in the first 6 months?
- What does success look like in the first 6-12 months?
- How much authority will the role have over engineering standards and resourcing?
- Is this more product engineering leadership or more hands-on delivery management?
- How does relocation support work in practice?

## Interview pack notes

- Hiring flow: screening -> video interview -> assessment -> final interview.
- For technical/specialist roles, they may use a case study, task, or psychometric test.
- They emphasize values alignment, team fit, and real examples of recent wins.
- There is a contact point in the pack: talent@pepperstone.com
