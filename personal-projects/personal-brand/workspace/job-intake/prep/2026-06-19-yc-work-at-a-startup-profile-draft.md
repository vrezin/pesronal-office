## Proud Project

One project I am proud of is moving GenAI customer-support automation from an experiment into production adoption on a high-load delivery/e-commerce platform.

The motivation was simple: support volume was growing with the business, and a classic FAQ chatbot would not be enough. We needed an AI system that could understand user intent, use internal context, call tools and APIs where appropriate, escalate to humans when confidence was low, and produce measurable operational impact without damaging customer experience.

My role was to shape the initiative from the engineering and architecture side: connect the business problem to practical AI use cases, define the system boundaries, work through the integration model with internal APIs and knowledge sources, and make sure the solution was treated as a production workflow rather than a demo. The architecture used an LLM as an orchestration layer around tools, internal data and escalation rules, with human-in-the-loop controls and quality monitoring.

The technical and organizational change mattered as much as the model choice. Technically, we had to design reliable integration points, guardrails, escalation paths, observability and feedback loops. Organizationally, support, product and engineering had to agree on ownership, acceptable risk, success metrics, and the operating process for improving the assistant after launch.

The result was production adoption where AI handled a large share of support requests, with human escalation for sensitive or uncertain cases and measurable six-figure quarterly savings. For me, the important lesson was that successful AI adoption is not "add a model"; it is product design, architecture, workflow integration, quality control and change management working together.
