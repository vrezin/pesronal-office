## Raw JD Summary

RapidSeedbox ищет fractional CTO для небольшой remote SaaS-команды: 2 инженера, CEO/PM как основной бизнес-контрагент. Главная задача - decouple legacy customer area на WHMCS/PHP в modern web app, провести 30-дневную technical/product diagnosis, сформировать 90-day execution plan, самому писать код/делать architecture/code review примерно 50% времени и одновременно выстроить delivery discipline, quality practices, AI-assisted development и product/tech roadmap.

- Built Fincomtech from zero: product vision, first engineering team, architecture, roadmap, pilots and enterprise presales.
- Operated Sobit as owner/director in high uncertainty: clients, requirements, architecture, finances and delivery.
- Led modernization and stabilization work in legacy/high-debt environments, including CDEK and Arameem/ToYou governance practices.
- Implemented engineering standards: unit testing, SonarQube, quality gates, release discipline, code review improvements and predictable delivery.
- Has GenAI/LLM and AI-assisted development positioning that matches their request to implement AI throughout the development cycle.

## Risks

- Risk 1: The role is very hands-on: "write code most days", 50% engineering, two technical interviews and AI-coding interview.
- Risk 2: Small team of 2 engineers may read 200+ people leadership as overqualified or insufficiently close to code.
- Risk 3: Specific legacy stack is WHMCS/PHP; direct recent PHP/WHMCS hands-on depth is not established in current profile.

## Risk Mitigation

- Mitigation 1: Position as "playing CTO": architecture, critical code paths, PR reviews, refactoring strategy, technical diagnosis, not detached executive management.
- Mitigation 2: Keep the first message practical and specific to their 30-day diagnosis and 90-day plan.
- Mitigation 3: Be transparent about stack: strong at diagnosing legacy systems and guiding safe decoupling; ready to go deep into PHP/WHMCS flows, billing dependencies and migration boundaries.

## Cover Letter

```text
Hi RapidSeedbox team,

Your challenge looks very familiar: a mature SaaS product with real customers, a legacy WHMCS/PHP customer area, billing dependencies, and the need to decouple safely without turning the rebuild into a long abstract modernization program.

I can be useful here because I combine hands-on technical leadership with product and delivery ownership:

- Built a product/engineering function from zero as a co-founder CTO: product vision, first team, architecture, roadmap, pilots and direct work with customers.
- Led legacy modernization and delivery governance in complex production environments, where the goal was not "rewrite everything", but reduce risk while keeping the business moving.
- Established engineering standards around code review, testing strategy, definition of done, quality gates and predictable release discipline.
- Worked directly with business/product owners to translate goals into milestones, sequencing, risks and realistic delivery plans.
- Implemented AI-assisted engineering and GenAI automation in production workflows, with quality control rather than uncontrolled speed.

I am comfortable with the fractional / playing CTO shape of the role: diagnosing the system, defining the architecture and migration plan, reviewing PRs, shipping critical parts myself, and helping a small team move faster without losing engineering quality.

The first thing I would want to understand is where WHMCS is currently most deeply coupled: billing flows, customer lifecycle, internal tools, permissions, support operations, or all of the above. That would define the safest first 90-day plan.
```

## Questions For Recruiter / Hiring Manager

- Question 1: What is the target stack for the new customer area, or is the CTO expected to choose it after the 30-day diagnosis?
- Question 2: Which WHMCS dependencies are currently most painful: billing, provisioning, customer portal, support, internal tooling, or reporting?
- Question 3: For the 4-hour/day format, what does success in the first 90 days mean: diagnosis and plan, first production slice, team operating model, or all of these?
