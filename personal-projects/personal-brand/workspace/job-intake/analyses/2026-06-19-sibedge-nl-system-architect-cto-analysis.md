## Risks

- The first task is explicitly `расшить монолит 1С`; a recruiter or hiring manager may over-index on direct 1C architecture experience.
- Office schedule is listed as 5/2, 09:00-18:00; remote/hybrid is not stated.
- Salary is hidden. The `5 000 руб` number is a product bonus, not compensation.
- NL International context may imply many business-process and legacy-enterprise politics around 1C, not only clean architecture work.
- Need clarify whether the role has real authority over teams/standards or only advisory responsibility without control.
- Need clarify exact fintech scope: payments, transaction processing, reconciliation, accounting, reporting, security, audit, reliability/SLA expectations.
- Need ensure "integration bus" is treated as a governed product/platform with owners, monitoring, contracts, security and roadmap, not as another unmanaged integration layer.
- Need clarify leadership mandate: team size, reporting line, hiring/budget, ability to change operating model, and who owns product priorities.

## Questions To Ask Early

1. Is direct hands-on 1C architecture/development a hard requirement, or is the expected role to lead decomposition/integration with 1C specialists in the team?
2. What is the compensation band for this role?
3. Is the work strictly office 5/2 in Novosibirsk, or is hybrid possible?
4. What authority does the role have over engineering teams, architecture standards, and technical debt prioritization?
5. What is the current system landscape around 1C: website/e-commerce, CRM, mobile/app channels, DWH/BI, integration layer, external APIs?
6. Who owns delivery and staffing today: NL internal team, Sibedge team, contractors, or mixed model?
7. What does success in the first 90 days look like: target architecture, migration roadmap, pilot extraction, integration bus, data layer MVP?
8. What are the main business pains: release speed, data quality, outages, integration bottlenecks, scaling, channel launch speed, reporting?
9. What exactly is expected from the first integration bus release: systems, flows, SLAs, monitoring, security, ownership, and success criteria?
10. What fintech processes are in the first contour: payments, accruals, transactions, accounting, reconciliation, reporting?
11. What is the intended IT operating model after separating IT into its own structure: product teams, platform teams, shared services, delivery governance, or another model?
12. Is the role expected to manage people and delivery directly, or influence through architecture only?
