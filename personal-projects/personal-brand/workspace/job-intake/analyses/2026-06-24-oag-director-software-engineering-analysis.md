## Risks

- Hybrid Vilnius is the main family/lifestyle gate.
- Domain is travel/flight-status intelligence; there may be expectations around real-time data depth and aviation data specifics.
- AI is supporting language, not the primary mandate.
- Salary `7500-11667 EUR` gross monthly may or may not clear target income after Lithuania taxes, housing, school, relocation, and family costs.

## Relocation / Lifestyle / Compensation

- Hybrid Vilnius would require a serious family relocation decision unless the employer accepts a different setup.
- Compensation is visible: `7500-11667 EUR` gross monthly. The high end may be worth testing, but after-tax family budget and relocation costs must be checked.
- Family logistics: Lithuania schools, housing, visas/residence, spouse/family adaptation, and travel cadence are open.
- Compared with remote-first roles, this is a heavier lifestyle change for less direct AI-agent positioning upside.

## Defuse Risks

- Ask location first: whether hybrid Vilnius is mandatory and whether relocation support exists.
- Ask compensation target and bonus/equity shape before any tailoring.
- Pitch through reliability, high-volume systems, engineering managers, AI-assisted product intelligence, and business-aligned delivery.

## Questions To Ask

- Is Vilnius hybrid mandatory, and what is the expected office cadence?
- Is relocation support offered for family moves?
- What is the realistic total compensation at senior Director level?
- How many teams and managers report to the role?
- How central are AI/ML capabilities to the first-year agenda?

## Next Action

Create a low-pressure active task to decide whether to test location/compensation. No tailored package until those gates are positive.
