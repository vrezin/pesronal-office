## Raw JD Summary

Early-stage CTO / technology partner for fintech/Web3 payment gateway and crypto processing platform. Need architecture from zero, hands-on development of key modules (address generation, transaction tracking, payment integrations, external APIs), security/fraud/double-spending, hiring, technical decisions. Strong plus/direct requirement: EVM/TRON transaction flows, smart contracts, security audits, key management, MPC wallets, own Web3 projects.

## Classification

- Primary track: Startup / Scale-up CTO
- Secondary track: Fintech / Payment architecture
- Tertiary track: Stability & Governance for reliability/security
- Seniority: hands-on founding CTO
- Role shape: early-stage technical co-founder / principal engineer + CTO
- Business context: Web3 payment gateway, crypto processing, pre-launch/pilot

## Best CV

- Recommended CV if applying: `CTO - Co-founder CTO - Head of Product Engineering.pdf`
- Alternative: none ideal.
- Do not use: Heavy Enterprise, Business Unit, Digital Transformation.

## Match Score

- Startup CTO / from zero: high
- Fintech / payment workflows: medium-high
- Highload / transaction reliability: high
- Hands-on coding key modules: medium-low risk
- Web3/EVM/TRON/smart contracts/MPC: low direct fit
- Compensation/risk: high uncertainty

## Evidence To Use

- Fincomtech: CTO/co-founder, product from zero, first engineering team, pilots.
- CFT: fintech/core banking.
- Arameem/ToYou: transactional platform, payment/settlement/accounting workflows, 1M+ financial transactions/day.
- Startup CV: product architecture, MVP, roadmap, enterprise presale.

## Risks

- They need hands-on Web3 infrastructure CTO, not just fintech architect.
- Direct EVM/TRON/smart contract/MPC experience not present.
- Fix + Option and pre-launch/pilot stage: financial risk.
- "minimal regulatory load" in payments/digital assets requires legal scrutiny.
- Could be founder-risk without enough cash.
- "Experience 3-6 years" is a seniority mismatch and may indicate they want a cheaper hands-on builder rather than an experienced CTO.
- "After round A - possible co-founder" means no real co-founder status now; option terms need hard scrutiny.
- Payment gateway + crypto processing + "minimal regulatory load" is a legal/compliance smell unless they can explain jurisdiction, custody model, AML/KYC, and liability.

## Recommendation

Verdict: risky / low priority by default.

Apply only if user is genuinely interested in Web3/payment startup risk and ready to be transparent about Web3 gap. This is not a clean CTO role; it reads like founding hands-on Web3 principal engineer + architecture + hiring + partner-facing CTO, with uncertain cash economics.

If applying:

- Use Startup CTO CV.
- Be explicit: strong fintech/transactional architecture and startup CTO, but Web3 infra needs fast ramp-up / can bring specialists.
- Do not claim smart contract/security audit experience if not true.
- Do not accept vague `Fix + Option`; ask runway, exact fixed compensation, vesting, cliff, option pool, and what happens before/after round A.

## Questions

- Какая часть fix гарантирована и на какой срок runway?
- Кто закрывает Web3/smart contract/security audit expertise сейчас?
- Какие сети и сценарии в MVP: EVM, TRON, stablecoins, fiat on/off ramp?
- Нужна ли custody/non-custody модель и кто отвечает за legal/regulatory design?
- Ожидается ли от CTO ежедневная разработка key modules или формирование команды?
- В какой юрисдикции запускается продукт и кто отвечает за AML/KYC/compliance?
- Что именно означает option: процент, vesting, cliff, strike/valuation, документы сейчас или "после раунда A"?
- Кто уже в founding team и кто принимает продуктовые/legal/security решения?
