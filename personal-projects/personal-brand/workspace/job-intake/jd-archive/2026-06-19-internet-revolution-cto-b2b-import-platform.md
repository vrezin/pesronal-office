## Company

Internet Revolution is an e-commerce company building and scaling its own products and brands on marketplaces.

The company manages the full chain from demand analytics and product development to production in China, international logistics and sales.

Signals from the JD:

- data-driven operating model;
- 1.5+ billion RUB revenue;
- compact team of about 70 people;
- existing 5-year internal infrastructure for sourcing, purchasing, logistics, storage and supply of goods from China.

## Product

The company wants to build a B2B platform where marketplace sellers can order goods from China turnkey: from product brief to marketplace warehouse delivery, with transparent cost, timing and SLA.

The product is described as an operating system for B2B import plus a storefront:

- sourcing;
- cost / TCO calculation;
- logistics and order execution;
- certification and document flow;
- marketplace and CRM integrations;
- seller cabinet and offer storefront.

Stage:

- zero stage;
- no code written yet;
- target: working model in 3 months;
- scaling in another 6 months.

## Role

The company is looking for a building CTO, not a scaling CTO.

Primary mission:

- build the technology platform and IT function from zero;
- keep technology as an accelerator for business and operations, not a resource sink;
- avoid overengineering while preserving evolutionary architecture.

## Responsibilities

### Hands-On Start

For the first 3-6 months the CTO is expected to:

- participate in writing key modules;
- do code review;
- set technical standards;
- delegate development after core team and delivery processes are established.

### Technology Strategy

- Build and defend technology strategy with CPO for a B2B wholesale platform targeting 500+ million RUB annual revenue.
- Roadmap for 12-24 months: MVP, pilot with 5-10 clients, scaling to dozens of active sellers and hundreds of orders per month.
- Manage IT portfolio across features, technical debt, integrations, BI and infrastructure.
- Define IT OKRs around time-to-market, stability, TCO, SLA and internal-user satisfaction.

### Architecture And Integrations

Core domains:

- Sourcing;
- Costing / TCO;
- Orders / Logistics;
- Docs / Certification;
- Billing;
- CRM;
- seller cabinet;
- offer storefront.

Integration layer:

- 1C / ERP / WMS;
- 1688 / Alibaba;
- WB / Ozon / Yandex Market;
- payment services;
- EDI / electronic document flow;
- BI / DWH.

The JD explicitly says the domain is not highload, but integration-heavy with complex business logic and slow external APIs.

Preferred approach:

- modular architecture with clean domain contracts at the start;
- evolve toward distributed system only after real load and usage patterns appear.

### Delivery, Operations, Quality

- Full delivery cycle from requirements to support.
- Target time-to-market for main features around 3 weeks.
- Availability and SLA for key services.
- Second-line support and incident response.
- Infrastructure TCO: cloud vs own infrastructure, containers, backups, cost control.

### Team And Process

Build IT department:

- first 3-4 months: backend, frontend, QA, DevOps, system architect;
- 4-6 months: BI, integrations, support, maybe R&D.

Process expectations:

- Scrum + Kanban;
- transparent metrics;
- hiring, onboarding, development, 1:1;
- responsibility system inside IT;
- OKR.

### Budget, Security, Reliability

- Own IT TCO, CAPEX/OPEX, infrastructure/subscription costs, investment justification and CEO reporting.
- Build-vs-buy for integrations.
- RBAC, SSO, audit log.
- Encryption, backups.
- Basic personal-data, marketplace and payment-system compliance.

## Technology Principles

- Domain modularity.
- API-first integration layer.
- Observability from day one: logs, metrics, tracing of key operations.
- Infrastructure as Code.
- Automated deployment and testing.
- Architecture should allow modules to be extracted into separate services later.

- Hands-on willingness at the start.
- 2+ years as CTO / Head of Engineering / VP Engineering, or 5+ years as strong Tech Lead / Staff Engineer / Head of Backend ready for C-level.
- Practical experience with integration-heavy systems and external APIs: ERP/1C, WMS, payments, marketplaces, CRM.
- Stack choice based on business context.
- Ability not to overengineer at the start.
- Mature technical-debt judgment.
- Team formation from zero.
