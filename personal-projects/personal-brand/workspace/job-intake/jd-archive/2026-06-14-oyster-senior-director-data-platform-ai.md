## JD Summary

Oyster is a global employment platform that helps companies hire, pay, and support talent anywhere. The company says it has a distributed team of 400+ people across 60+ countries, raised USD 286m, reached a USD 1.2b valuation, and became a B Corp in 2023.

The Senior Director, Data Platform and AI owns the technical infrastructure and strategic direction for turning Oyster into an AI-native global platform. The role is part of the Senior Tech Leadership Team and combines data platforms, product analytics pipelines, knowledge management, LLM orchestration, MLOps, and advanced automation frameworks.

## Responsibilities

- Drive the company-wide AI agenda from local experiments into centralized production systems.
- Own broad AI initiatives with cross-functional impact across customer product, internal workflows, and core processes.
- Partner with Product, Engineering, People, Operations, and other leaders to embed AI into business workflows.
- Serve as the technical authority for data and AI infrastructure, including MLOps pipelines, LLM orchestration frameworks, and distributed data systems.
- Oversee a scalable corporate data platform as the foundation for AI and machine learning capabilities.
- Transform internal knowledge architecture so unstructured information becomes searchable and actionable.
- Build data structures and connectivity pipelines optimized for AI model deployment, operation, and maintenance.
- Set tooling standards, technical guardrails, and engineering practices for embedded AI specialists across business units.
- Support decentralized specialists by giving them centralized platform support and implementation help.
- Maintain ethical compliance, data privacy, platform security, and governance across AI initiatives.
- Educate and empower teams to scale AI use productively.
- Lead change management and literacy work that shifts daily workflows from manual work to AI-assisted operations.

## Requirements

- Genuine passion for AI beyond product feature delivery, with evidence of internal organizational change around AI workflows and habits.
- Experience in a service-delivery model or complex operational business where data strategy affects real-world workflows and diverse stakeholders.
- Background weighted toward data engineering, distributed systems, platform services, or system architecture.
- Deep technical track record in distributed data systems, MLOps pipelines, and LLM orchestration.
- Experience building and scaling asynchronous data structures, ML routing layers, or high-volume API integrations.
- Ability to design AI/data solutions across interconnected technical platforms, business processes, and human teams.
- Track record of driving internal adoption for major technology or workflow shifts.
- Familiarity with model validation, anomaly logging, data pipeline health metrics, and automated governance.
- Bonus: semantic search, internal RAG layers, or zero-to-one multi-tenant customer data structures.
