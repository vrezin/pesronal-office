# Job Search Operating System

Дата актуализации: 2026-06-10

This note answers a simple question: if competence and personal brand are already fixed, what else is needed for the search to actually work?

## Already In Place

- core competence story;
- personal brand / market positioning;
- final CV portfolio;
- job-intake archive and analysis flow;
- relocation cards and country filters;
- Gmail / LinkedIn monitoring automation;
- interview prep notes for active roles.

## Still Needed For A Real Search Machine

### 1. Search Policy

- when to apply;
- when to skip;
- when to tailor;
- when to stop after the first signal;
- when to treat a vacancy as relocation-only or no-go.

### 2. Market Map

- target countries and cities;
- target company types;
- target role families;
- which markets are English-friendly vs language-heavy;
- which markets are worth the family friction.

### 3. Economics Layer

- minimum acceptable salary;
- target salary by market;
- tax and net-income impact;
- housing and school cost;
- relocation package threshold;
- self-relocation viability.

### 4. Evidence Layer

- measurable outcomes;
- project examples;
- leadership scope;
- scale stories;
- relocation/family-fit context where relevant;
- references or social proof where available.

### 5. Outreach Layer

- which CV to send;
- whether to use a tailored copy;
- whether to attach a cover letter;
- what to say in first contact;
- follow-up cadence after no response;
- how to react to a request for salary, relocation, or notice-period details.

### 6. Monitoring Layer

- HH inbox;
- LinkedIn inbox;
- direct referral messages;
- recruiter replies;
- vacancy digests;
- active lead list by country and company.

### 7. Conversion Layer

- screening answers;
- interview prep briefs;
- company notes;
- country notes;
- objection handling;
- post-interview follow-up.

### 8. Review Loop

- track response rate;
- track interview rate;
- track offer rate;
- track no-response patterns;
- update the rules when a pattern repeats.

## Practical Interpretation

If competence is the engine and personal brand is the body, then the missing parts are:

- the steering wheel: search policy;
- the map: market and relocation filters;
- the fuel gauge: economics;
- the sensors: monitoring and evidence;
- the gearbox: outreach and conversion;
- the maintenance log: review loop.

## Immediate Priority Order

1. finalize search decision rules;
2. finalize relocation/country cards;
3. set economic thresholds;
4. keep inbox monitoring running;
5. build a repeatable outreach cadence;
6. measure what actually converts.
