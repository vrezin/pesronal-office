# Selected Cases

Дата: 2026-06-10

This file is a reusable case bank for resume builders, interview prep, LinkedIn posts, and recruiter conversations.

## How To Use

- In a CV, keep only the short case title and 1-2 bullets.
- In an interview, use the full STAR structure.
- In LinkedIn, expand the `Action` and `Result` sections into a post.
- Prefer the case that matches the vacancy family: startup, enterprise, transformation, AI, or founder/operator.

## 1. Launching a fintech platform from scratch

### Best For

- Startup CTO
- Co-founder CTO
- Head of Product Engineering
- AI-first startup / platform roles

### Short CV Version

- Co-founded Fincomtech and took the first platform from concept to pilot deployments.
- Built the first engineering team, delivery model, cloud foundation, and release control.
- Worked directly with customers and enterprise presales to convert business needs into roadmap and implementation scope.

### STAR

- **Situation:** The company was being created from zero, with no mature product, no engineering team, and no established delivery process.
- **Task:** Turn the business idea into a working fintech platform, build the first engineering foundation, and validate whether the product could be delivered to real customers.
- **Action:** I shaped the product vision, hired and organized the initial engineering team, set up prioritization and release control, defined the cloud and DevOps foundation, and worked directly with first customers and enterprise presales to translate business needs into a realistic roadmap and pilot scope.
- **Result:** The platform moved from concept to pilot deployments, proving both technical feasibility and commercial viability.

## 2. Scaling a high-load delivery platform and engineering org

### Best For

- Heavy Enterprise
- VP Engineering
- Director of Engineering
- Platform / governance roles

### Short CV Version

- Led a distributed engineering organization of 200+ people and 14 leads across engineering, QA, and DevOps.
- Scaled the platform from 30K to 300K daily active users and from 100 to 100K+ orders/day, with peaks of 130K.
- Built transactional and financial workflows handling 1M+ financial transactions/day.

### STAR

- **Situation:** A fast-growing delivery platform needed to scale its engineering, transaction handling, and operational governance while the business expanded rapidly.
- **Task:** Keep the platform reliable and scalable, maintain delivery pace, and build a leadership layer that could support growth without breaking production.
- **Action:** I led a distributed engineering organization of 200+ people, managed 14 team leads, introduced portfolio visibility and prioritization discipline, organized release governance, and built stronger quality controls around testing, ownership, and incidents. I also worked on the transactional architecture, including payment, commission, payouts, subscriptions, and BNPL flows.
- **Result:** The platform scaled to 300K DAU and 100K+ orders/day, with peaks of 130K, while handling 1M+ financial transactions/day and cutting cloud costs by more than 50%.

## 3. Stabilizing a legacy logistics platform

### Best For

- Transformation roles
- Stability & Governance
- Legacy modernization
- Enterprise delivery leadership

### Short CV Version

- Modernized and stabilized CDEK’s core logistics platform after years of technical debt.
- Improved performance by more than 50% through architectural redesign and database optimization.
- Reduced outages by redefining ownership and separating support from development work.

### STAR

- **Situation:** The core logistics platform had accumulated more than a decade of technical debt, with instability and performance bottlenecks affecting delivery.
- **Task:** Stabilize production, improve performance, and create a more sustainable engineering model without stopping business operations.
- **Action:** I led a modernization effort that included architectural refactoring, database optimization, clearer ownership boundaries, and a separate support line so development could focus on product evolution. I also introduced more structured engineering processes and release discipline.
- **Result:** The platform became stable, performance improved by more than 50%, outages dropped, and the team gained a more sustainable operating model.

## 7. Designing an AI-native delivery system

### Best For

- AI-first CTO
- AI platform / workflow roles
- Product engineering with governance
- Case-study interviews

### Short CV Version

- Designed an AI-native SDLC with specs, task artifacts, context management, agents, guardrails, validators, and diff discipline.
- Treated chat as an intake mode, not the product center.
- Built workflow-first execution around owned context and traceability.

### STAR

- **Situation:** AI-assisted development was becoming possible, but most teams were still using it in an ad hoc and poorly controlled way.
- **Task:** Turn AI from a vague productivity gimmick into a managed delivery system with traceability and quality.
- **Action:** I defined an AI-native SDLC: clear specification artifacts, explicit task/context boundaries, agent roles, guardrails, validators, and review discipline. I treated chat as one mode of intake, not the operating system itself.
- **Result:** The delivery model became more structured, reviewable, and suitable for real engineering workflows instead of one-off experimentation.

## 9. Recovering delivery after a key outsourcing team collapse

### Best For

- Heavy Enterprise
- Transformation
- Stability & Governance
- Leadership / crisis-management interviews

### Short CV Version

- Recovered the delivery organization after a key outsourced team abruptly left and blocked QA, ERP, and CRM development.
- Rebuilt the lost capability through direct outreach, HR coordination, and rapid team reconfiguration.
- Restored delivery continuity without critical incidents or major project damage during the transition, and ended up with a lower-cost setup than the previous outsourced model.

### STAR

- **Situation:** A key outsourcing partner left the project abruptly, and the delivery organization lost more than 30 people at once across QA, internal ERP, and CRM development. That created an immediate blockage in critical delivery streams.
- **Task:** Restore delivery capability fast enough to keep the business running, avoid production incidents, and rebuild the missing engineering capacity.
- **Action:** I activated my own network, worked closely with HR, contacted each departing specialist directly, negotiated the recovery path, and rebuilt the team in a slightly different in-house configuration instead of depending on the same brittle outsourcing model.
- **Result:** Within about 1.5 months, the team returned in a new in-house shape, critical delivery continued, and there were no critical incidents or major project failures during the transition.
- **Result:** Within about 1.5 months, the team returned in a new in-house shape, critical delivery continued, there were no critical incidents or major project failures during the transition, and the resulting setup was cheaper than the previous outsourced arrangement.
