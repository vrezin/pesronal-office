# Почему AI пугает управленцев: он делает компетентность видимой

Status: outline / sensitive theme / publish with caution  
Author: Vladimir Rezin  
Publication owner: Vladimir Rezin personal brand  
Potential channel: personal LinkedIn; likely personal-only, not corporate, unless softened heavily.

## Raw Thought

One barrier to AI adoption, especially in hierarchical organizations, may be that AI makes visible the incompetence of people whose authority was protected by opacity.

The initial emotional framing was about the older generation being afraid that AI will expose their incompetence. This should be handled carefully: the stronger and safer version is not "older people are incompetent", but:

> AI threatens status structures built on opacity, ritualized experience, slow information flow, and decision control.

This often correlates with seniority and older management layers, but the real variable is not age. It is whether a person's authority depends on actual competence or on control of process, access, signatures, and ambiguity.

## Core Thesis

AI adoption is difficult not only because of security, data, hallucinations or regulation.

It is difficult because AI changes the visibility of competence.

When AI can quickly summarize documents, compare positions, expose contradictions, draft options, prepare risks, build checklists, and ask the obvious missing questions, it becomes harder to hide behind:

- "we need more time to understand";
- "let's discuss later";
- "this is complicated";
- "we need to align";
- "according to the process";
- "only experienced people understand this";
- "I have been doing this for 20 years".

AI does not automatically make junior people wiser.

But it reduces the information asymmetry that protected weak decision-makers.

## Stronger Framing

The fear is not that AI will replace managers.

The fear is that AI will reveal which managers were adding judgment, and which managers were mostly adding delay.

## Why This Is Sensitive

Avoid making it purely generational.

Better distinctions:

- competence-based authority vs status-based authority;
- decision ownership vs decision theater;
- experience as judgment vs experience as shield;
- governance as risk management vs governance as self-protection.

## Possible Opening

Мы часто обсуждаем страх сотрудников перед AI: людей заменят, профессии исчезнут, junior-задачи автоматизируются.

Но мне кажется, есть менее обсуждаемый страх.

AI пугает не только исполнителей.

AI пугает управленческие слои, чья власть держалась на непрозрачности.

Не потому что AI лучше принимает решения.

А потому что AI быстрее показывает, кто вообще способен эти решения принимать.

## Key Points

1. In slow organizations, incompetence can hide inside process.
   - slow meetings;
   - long alignment;
   - unclear ownership;
   - documents nobody reads;
   - status-based authority.

2. AI compresses preparation work.
   - summaries;
   - contradictions;
   - options;
   - risks;
   - decision maps;
   - RACI;
   - acceptance criteria.

3. Once preparation is faster, the remaining delay is more visible.
   - Who is blocking?
   - Who cannot decide?
   - Who does not understand the subject?
   - Who uses process as cover?

4. This threatens status-based managers.
   - They are not afraid of AI writing code.
   - They are afraid of AI making their lack of judgment visible.

5. Real experience still matters.
   - AI makes strong experts stronger;
   - it helps people with judgment move faster;
   - but it exposes people whose only asset is opacity.

6. This may explain resistance to AI adoption.
   - security and data concerns are real;
   - but some resistance will be dressed as risk management while actually defending status.

## Final Thesis

AI will not remove the need for experienced leaders.

But it will separate experience from status.

And that may be one of the most uncomfortable parts of AI transformation.
