## Interpretation

The weak point is not only invitation conversion. The larger problem is low passive discovery: HH shows hundreds of "matching" vacancies, but resume views are low and invitations are almost absent.

This suggests that passive HH resume exposure is a weak channel for the current senior / cross-domain positioning. It should not be treated as the main source of near-term income.

Observed pattern:

- AI Transformation has the most views but zero invitations so far.
- Stability & Governance and Startup / Product Engineering are the only tracks with invitations.
- Business Unit / Integration and Digital Transformation have very low views, despite being strategically important tracks.

## Operational Consequence

Do not spend emotional energy trying to manually optimize every HH vacancy. Use HH as:

- a source of signals and occasional direct applications;
- not the primary lead-generation engine;
- not a place for heavy ATS polishing before a human reply.

Near-term focus should shift toward:

1. live processes and responses;
2. direct / warm outreach;
3. a sharper cashflow-first service offer;
4. a small experiment on HH resume packaging, not endless vacancy-by-vacancy tailoring.

The sharper problem is post-view conversion:

- the resume gets opened by relevant companies;
- invitations do not follow;
- therefore the likely weak point is the first screen / title / role-shape / perceived seniority / perceived hands-on fit after the employer opens the resume.

This is a different problem from "HH does not show the resume". It means the next experiment should not be broad vacancy digging; it should be a small packaging test for the AI resume's first screen.

## Second Refined Interpretation

The detailed view list should not be read as strong passive market demand.

More accurate diagnosis:

- AI resume gets opened mostly after applications / HH interactions;
- organic inbound discovery appears weak or unproven;
- post-application conversion is also weak: many employers view, then do not invite;
- therefore HH is mainly a response-tracking and vacancy-sourcing channel, not a reliable inbound lead channel.

This reduces the value of "raise resume and wait" as a strategy. It also reduces the value of maintaining many parallel HH resume variants.

Next experiment should be bounded:

1. Improve the AI resume first screen for post-application conversion.
2. Track only applications where the employer viewed the resume and then either invited/rejected/ignored.
3. Separate `organic view` from `application-driven view` in future snapshots.

## Anti-Strategy: Mass Clicker

User considered whether to run a clicker that blindly applies to the roughly 4,000 vacancies HH shows as somewhat relevant across the six resumes.

Decision: do not do this as the main strategy.

Why:

- it increases rejection/noise volume without improving the matching logic;
- it destroys signal quality: after mass apply, it becomes harder to understand which titles, resumes, industries and messages work;
- it likely trains the user into an exhausting slot-machine loop;
- HH already shows that many "matching" vacancies are false positives or downlevel/misaligned;
- application-driven views without invitations are already weak, so multiplying low-quality applications is unlikely to solve the actual bottleneck.

If automation is used at all, it should be a scorer, not a clicker:

- fetch / parse vacancy;
- classify A/B/C;
- apply only B+ above a threshold;
- log resume used, cover text, view status, and outcome;
- cap daily applications to a small number so the queue remains human-auditable.

## Recommended Next Mode

For the next sprint, run a controlled channel mix:

1. `A-class`: live human processes and strong direct matches. Full attention allowed.
2. `B-class`: fast template applications, capped.
3. `C-class`: ignored unless explicitly reopened.
4. Direct/warm outreach: prioritized over more HH digging.
5. Cashflow offer: create and send a short paid consulting/audit offer to warm contacts.

Suggested cap:

- HH: no more than 5-10 B-class applications per day;
- no custom resume before a human reply;
- review conversion weekly, not continuously;
- if a channel produces views but no invitations, change packaging or channel, not volume.
