### 1) Example Of AI Initiative Moved From Experiment To Company-Wide Adoption

One concrete example is AI-assisted support automation in a production customer-support workflow. What started as a limited experiment with LLM-based assistance became a broader operating capability after we made the system reliable enough for real daily use.

Technically, the main changes were:

- moved from a demo-style prompt flow to a governed orchestration layer;
- connected the LLM to internal APIs and tools instead of relying on chat-only output;
- added human escalation for uncertain or risky cases;
- introduced quality gates, review points, and measurable success criteria;
- treated observability, error handling, and traceability as first-class requirements.

Organizationally, the biggest changes were:

- shifted from “try AI here and there” to a defined operating process;
- aligned support, engineering, and management on where AI could act and where a human stayed responsible;
- created ownership for the workflow instead of leaving it as an isolated pilot;
- trained the team to use the new flow consistently and to trust it only within clear boundaries;
- measured impact in operational terms rather than as a novelty.

The key lesson was that adoption only happens when AI stops being a side experiment and becomes part of the team’s real workflow, with clear guardrails and ownership.
