## Context

The job-search and CV workflow is already producing a lot of useful artifacts, but the decision rules are still partially implicit and keep resurfacing in monitor logs and live discussions.

Current backlog items include:

- when to avoid applying at all;
- when to use the standard HH CV vs a tailored copy;
- how aggressively to hide early career history;
- when to keep `23 years` vs downshift to `12-15 years`;
- how to phrase hands-on leadership without sounding downgraded;
- how to evaluate tech-stack gaps;
- how to treat Russian vs international market differences;
- how to classify integrator and business-unit roles;
- how to account for cost-of-living and relocation economics.

Create a practical decision matrix and short policy note that can be reused during vacancy intake and application preparation.

2. A concise decision matrix covering:
   - apply / skip / tailor;

   - tech-stack gap thresholds;
   - Russian vs international market logic;
   - integrator/business-unit classification;

3. A short “if unsure” fallback section for future vacancy analyses.

## Next Step

Done for the first practical version.

The reusable rule is now the `A-class / B-class / C-class` effort gate:

- `A-class`: rare strong fit or live human process; careful thinking and tailoring are allowed.
- `B-class`: plausible role; use a ready CV and short template, no targeted resume before human reply.
- `C-class`: maybe / market signal / weak practical path; park unless explicitly reopened.
